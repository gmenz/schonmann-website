Roberto H. Schonmann is Emeritus Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of California, Los Angeles. He joined UCLA as Associate Professor in 1990, became Full Professor in 1992, served as Graduate Vice-Chair from 1997 to 1999, and retired effective July 2009.

His work spans probability theory and mathematical physics, including the contact process, bootstrap percolation, metastability of stochastic Ising models, large deviations, and percolation on non-amenable graphs. Since retirement his research has continued into the evolution of cooperation, economics and finance, and the foundations of quantum mechanics.

Research interests

  • Probability theory and statistical mechanics
  • Evolution of cooperation by natural selection
  • Economics and finance
  • Foundations of quantum mechanics

Selected honors

  • Fellow, John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation (1999–2000)
  • Invited 45-minute speaker, International Congress of Mathematicians, Berlin (1998)
  • Fellow, Institute of Mathematical Statistics (1996)
